# Interview Scorecard

Optimize raw interview notes into a clean, structured Ashby scorecard.

## Input

- Raw interview notes from the user, typically unstructured bullet points and observations

## Output Format

Structure the scorecard into three sections:

### Pro

- One-dimensional bullet points
- Format: **`<Keyword Summary>:`** `<Description>`
- Clean up grammar, remove redundancy, sharpen language
- Keep the original meaning and observations intact
- Each bullet should cover exactly one topic

### Con

- One-dimensional bullet points
- Format: **`<Keyword Summary>:`** `<Description>`
- Same rules as Pro
- Preserve the user's own assessment and nuance — don't over-dramatize or soften concerns

### Wrap-Up

- Short, concise paragraphs
- Keep Denis's casual but professional tone
- Summarize the overall recommendation and key impressions
- Lead with the hire/no-hire signal

## Conventions

- Do not invent observations — only refine what the user provided
- Preserve the user's judgment calls and qualifications (e.g. "I still think that's fine")
- Fix typos and grammar but keep the voice natural
- Merge related sub-points into single cohesive bullets where it improves clarity
- Keep keyword summaries to 1-3 words
