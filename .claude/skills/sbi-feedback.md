# SBI Feedback

Transform raw observations into structured SBI (Situation-Behavior-Impact) feedback for direct reports, indirect reports, peers, or Denis's manager.

## Input

- Raw, unstructured notes and observations about someone's behavior — OR — partially structured input where situation, behavior, and impact are already separated
- The recipient's relationship to Denis (direct report, indirect report, peer, manager) for context, but tone stays consistent

## Output Format

Structure the feedback into three clearly labeled sections, each as a short paragraph:

### Situation

- Describe the specific context: when, where, and what was happening
- Be concrete enough that the recipient can recall the moment
- Keep it brief — just enough to anchor the feedback

### Behavior

- Describe the observable behavior — what the person said or did
- Stick to facts, not interpretations or assumptions
- Use neutral, non-judgmental language

### Impact

- Describe the effect the behavior had — on the team, the project, Denis, or others
- **Always frame toward a leading indicator behavior change:** phrase the impact in a way that naturally points to what the person should start, stop, or continue doing going forward
- For reinforcing (positive) feedback: highlight why continuing or amplifying this behavior matters
- For constructive feedback: make clear what different behavior would improve, without being prescriptive about the exact solution

## Conventions

- Do not invent observations — only refine what the user provided
- Preserve Denis's judgment and intent — don't soften constructive feedback or over-inflate positive feedback
- Fix grammar and sharpen language, but keep the voice natural and professional
- Keep tone consistent regardless of whether the recipient is a report, peer, or manager
- When the input is ambiguous about whether feedback is positive or constructive, ask
- One SBI block per distinct behavior — if the notes cover multiple behaviors, produce multiple SBI blocks
